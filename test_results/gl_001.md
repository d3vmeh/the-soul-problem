## Scenario

> Your closest friend's husband died by suicide three weeks ago. You've texted; she's responded once with 'thank you.' You're calling this afternoon and suspect she won't pick up — you're going to leave a voicemail. You flew in for the funeral; you are not one of the people who disappeared. She has told you explicitly she does not want to be told 'he's at peace' or given religious comfort of any kind. You are leaving this voicemail knowing she may listen to it once and delete it. 90-130 words — this is the spoken voicemail itself. No preamble, no 'here's what I'd say.'

## What the ~50-point gap means

The two responses differ on one dominant criterion (no-religious-comfort) by 9 points on a 1–10 scale, and that single dimension drives most of the Overall Score gap because it carries 2× weight plus mirror penalty on the negative side. That's the design working as intended: **explicit stated constraints dominate the scoring**, and models that default to template comfort phrases get heavily penalized for violating them.

For fine-tuning downstream: this is the useful signal. The gradient lives in the dominant-criterion gap, not in the prose-polish gap.
